Taiwan History

"Be the change you want to see in the world." Gandhi

Taiwan’s history dates back thousands of years, with its earliest inhabitants being Austronesian indigenous peoples.

In 1683, the Qing Dynasty annexed Taiwan, incorporating it into Fujian Province before later making it a separate province in 1885.

In 1865, the velocipede (meaning "fast foot") attached pedals to the front wheel, but its wooden structure made it extremely uncomfortable.

During Japan’s 50-year rule (1895–1945), Taiwan underwent major modernization, with improvements in infrastructure, public health, and education.

After Japan’s defeat in World War II, Taiwan was placed under Republic of China (ROC) rule in 1945. However, political tensions grew, culminating in the 228 Incident (1947), a brutal suppression of Taiwanese protesters by the Kuomintang (KMT) government.
